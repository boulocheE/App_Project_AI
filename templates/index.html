Parcours #1

Welcome to my Data Science and Artificial Intelligence portfolio. I'm delighted that you're visiting my website. This platform serves as an opportunity for me to showcase a variety of projects, each with its unique characteristics. While some projects represent significant efforts spanning several weeks, others are smaller endeavors requiring only a few hours of work. I invite you to explore, share, and offer your feedback.

This portfolio serves as a gateway to the various projects I undertook during the "Artificial Intelligence in Data Science" course. As a French student, I had the unique opportunity to participate in the ERASMUS program during the autumn semester of 2023-2024. My studies took place at the Budapest University of Technology and Economics in Budapest, where I gained extensive knowledge in various aspects of Artificial Intelligence. This experience not only allowed me to immerse myself in Eastern European culture but also provided me with the chance to explore neighboring countries like Romania, Serbia, and Austria. Additionally, it offered a fresh perspective on programming, introducing me to different standards and practices prevalent in other countries. This journey was not just an academic pursuit but also a deep dive into each domain that captivates my interest.

Parcours #3

During the "Artificial Intelligence in Data Science" course, I had the chance to delve into numerous facets of programming, each offering a unique perspective and set of skills. The course covered a broad range of topics, including:

  • Statistical Overview: A foundational understanding of statistics in the context of AI.
  • Image Segmentation and Recognition: Focused on tasks like car plate recognition and number recognition, applying advanced image processing techniques.
  • Deep Learning: Building neural networks from scratch using numpy, and exploring deeper concepts in subsequent classes.
  • Titanic Data Analysis: A practical application involving the fitting of Titanic dataset, showcasing data handling and analysis skills.
  • Higher-Level Implementations: Utilizing frameworks like TensorFlow and sklearn for more complex tasks, including one hot encoding and dataset handling (e.g., MNIST dataset).
  • Challenges and Competitions: Engaging in practical challenges like the Red Wine challenge, where we applied our knowledge to real-world scenarios.
  • Advanced Neural Network Concepts: Exploring convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for tasks like shape recognition and handwritten digit recognition, including techniques like early stopping, training visualization, and callbacks.
  • Utilizing Pre-Trained Models: Learning about the application and modification of pre-trained models, such as freezing layers and adapting models like VGG16 for specific tasks.
  • Data Augmentation and Autoencoding: Techniques to enhance and create more versatile datasets, applied in contexts like augmenting handwritten digits data.
  • Genetic Algorithms and Reinforcement Learning: Implementing AI in games, evidenced by projects where AI plays games like Snake, Flappy Bird, and Tic-Tac-Toe, and analyzing their learning and adaptation processes.
  • Handling Textual and Sequential Data: Projects included predicting missing words from Harry Potter sentences and advanced synonym analysis, as well as using LSTM for airline and word prediction, and temperature prediction.
  • Unsupervised Learning and Decision Trees: Focusing on techniques like random forest and other unsupervised learning methods.

This comprehensive curriculum not only enhanced my technical prowess in various AI domains but also provided a holistic view of how these technologies can be applied in diverse scenarios.
